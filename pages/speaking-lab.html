Speaking Lab

Master the art of public speaking with interactive exercises and techniques used by world-class debaters.

Volume

Appropriate volume ensures you are heard and respected.

Tips

Beginner 3 min

The Breath Foundation

Proper breathing is the foundation of good vocal projection and helps reduce nervousness.

  • 1 Stand or sit with good posture
  • 2 Breathe in for 4 counts through nose
  • 3 Hold for 2 counts
  • 4 Breathe out for 6 counts through mouth
  • 5 Repeat 5 times before speaking
Beginner 5 min

Tongue Twisters

Improve articulation and clarity under pressure.

  • 1 Start: "Peter Piper picked a peck..."
  • 2 Increase speed while keeping clarity
  • 3 Try: "She sells seashells by the seashore"
  • 4 Repeat each 5 times
Intermediate 5 min

Volume Scaling

Control volume dynamically without straining.

  • 1 Choose a simple sentence
  • 2 Say at whisper level (1/10)
  • 3 Increase to conversational (5/10)
  • 4 Push to presentation level (7/10)
Advanced 5 min

The Pause Power

Master strategic silence to create impact.

  • 1 Prepare a 30-second argument
  • 2 Pause 2s before main claim
  • 3 Pause 1s after evidence
  • 4 Practice until natural

Opening Statement

"Ladies and gentlemen, today we stand at a crossroads. The decision before us will shape our community for generations to come. I urge you to consider not just the immediate benefits, but the lasting legacy we will leave."
Focus: Practice building dramatic tension and using pauses for effect.

Evidence Presentation

"According to a 2023 study by Harvard University, 78% of participants showed significant improvement when given access to these resources. This isn't just a marginal gain—it's a transformation."
Focus: Emphasize statistics and source credibility.

Logical Argument

"If we accept that education improves outcomes, and we accept that outcomes drive economic growth, then we must conclude that investing in education is investing in our economy."
Focus: Maintain clarity and use pace to help audience follow logic.
